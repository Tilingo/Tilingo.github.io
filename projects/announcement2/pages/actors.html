Hugo Weaving

Hugo Wallace Weaving was born in Ibadan, Nigeria, on April 4, 1960. He played Agent Smith in the Matrix film trilogy and Elrond in The Lord of the Rings film trilogy. Often cast as a villain, Weaving has had starring roles in Transformers (2007), Captain America (2011) and Cloud Atlas (2012).

Natalie Portman

Born in Israel in 1981, actress Natalie Portman grew up on Long Island, New York, and began modeling at age 11. Her film debut was in The Professional (1994), and she was cast as Queen Amidala in the Star Wars prequels. While continuing her career, Portman earned a degree in psychology from Harvard University. She won the 2010 Best Actress Oscar for her portrayal of a troubled ballerina in Black Swan. In 2012, she married dancer Benjamin Millepied, with whom she has two children. John Hurt

John Hurt was born January 22, 1940 in Chesterfield, Derbyshire, England. He made his film and stage debuts in 1962. He had notable roles in A Man for All Seasons (1966), The Naked Servant (1977), Midnight Express (1978), and The Elephant Man (1980); for the latter two movies he received Academy Award nominations. The prolific actor also appeared in Alien (1979), Heaven's Gate (1980), The Osterman Weekend (1983), The Hit (1984), the 1984 film adaptation of George Orwell's 1984, three Harry Potter films and the biopic Jackie. In 2012, the British Academy of Film and Television Arts honored Hurt with a lifetime achievement award. He died on January 27, 2017 at the age of 77 after battling pancreatic cancer.
